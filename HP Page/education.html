Phenomena

Synthetic Biology Nowadays

The revolutionary and disruptive innovation potential of synthetic biology has become a strategic highland of science and technology that countries around the world are vying for, and is triggering a new round of international competition in science, technology and industry.

Countries and regions such as the United States, the United Kingdom, Australia and the European Union have continuously updated and released relevant research and technology roadmaps, increased funding and continuously supported new research projects, and established synthetic biology/engineering biology research centers and platform facilities. Against the backdrop of huge R&D and industrial transformation efforts, the applications of synthetic biology are rapidly expanding to important socioeconomic fields such as materials and energy, as well as fields related to people's health such as medicine, agriculture and food, forming an emerging "industrial direction" and even possibly an emerging "investment ecosystem".

The 2022 China Synthetic Biology Green Application and Industry Perception Research Report shows that 57.81% of the respondents are optimistic about the development of synthetic biology technology in the next 10 years.

This implies that there are also some factors affecting the public's acceptance of synthetic biology. For example, in terms of scientific understanding, as an emerging field, the scientific understanding of synthetic biology is still in the development stage. In contrast, although transgenic technology has undergone a lot of research, due to its long development history and complex issues involved, the public has different views on its safety and reliability. Some people are skeptical about technology, especially genetic technology, and worry that it may disrupt the natural balance and weaken the original quality of organisms. Media reports also have a certain influence on the public's perception. The reports of mass media on transgenic crops are mostly negative and warning, emphasizing potential risks and uncertainties, which may deepen the public's rejection and anxiety about related technologies.

Public Biography

Once the questionnaire data is collected, the following steps can be taken to evaluate the understanding of synthetic biology among the population and plan further measures:

Data Analysis

1. Calculate the percentages of different responses for each question. For example, in the question about age groups, determine the proportion of respondents in each category. This will help understand the demographic distribution of those participating in the survey.

2. Analyze the responses regarding knowledge of synthetic biology. Look at the percentage of people who have heard of it and those who can accurately describe its applications. This will give an indication of the overall awareness level.

Interpretation

1. Based on the data analysis, identify areas where there is a lack of understanding. If a large number of respondents cannot name specific applications of synthetic biology, it indicates a knowledge gap in that area.

2. Determine the main concerns and sources of worry among those who are skeptical. This will help in addressing these specific issues in future communication.

3. Look for trends in the data. For example, if a particular age group or educational background shows a more positive or negative attitude, this can be further explored.

Communication and Education

1. Develop educational materials based on the identified knowledge gaps. Create brochures, online articles, or videos that explain synthetic biology in simple terms and address the common misconceptions.

2. Organize public seminars or webinars to present the facts about synthetic biology and answer questions from the audience. Invite experts in the field to share their insights.

3. Use social media platforms to disseminate accurate information and engage in discussions with the public. Respond to comments and queries to clarify any misunderstandings.

Policy and Regulation

Based on the public's concerns and attitudes, provide input to policymakers. Suggest measures to ensure the safe development and application of synthetic biology. Advocate for more research funding to further explore the potential benefits and risks of synthetic biology. This will help in making more informed decisions.

Action

Students

Children 1-15

We will organize a series of workshops tailored for children aged 1 - 15. These workshops will be filled with fun-filled activities such as game-based learning sessions. We plan to design a "Hair Health Scavenger Hunt" game, where children will search for pictures or objects related to healthy hair habits. This game will not only make learning about hair care an enjoyable experience but also help them remember key points about keeping their hair healthy, like the importance of regular washing and a balanced diet.

Figure 1: Example Version of Comics

We'll also create a comic book series focused on hair health. These comics will feature colorful characters going on adventures related to hair care. Through the stories, children will learn about the basics of hair growth and how to prevent common hair problems. The comic books will be distributed in schools and community centers. This way, we can plant the seeds of hair health awareness in young minds at an early stage, setting a good foundation for their future hair care routines.

Teens 15-18

For teenagers aged 15 - 18, we'll launch a remote-teaching-based synthetic biology education program. We'll recruit college students majoring in relevant fields or professionals in the Biology or Medicine to be remote teachers. They will conduct online classes about the science behind hair loss and effective prevention methods. This not only provides teenagers with in-depth knowledge but also offers them a chance to interact with experts in the field, inspiring their interest in science and health-related subjects. Actually, we have already started the program in winter and decided to continue this adventure.

We conducted a total of 15-hour online teaching courses at Haixi Middle School in Haixi, Qinghai. The courses mainly explored the principles, applications, and real-world cases of synthetic biology, as well as the specific design mechanism of our winter project, MEDUSA.

During the summer, we'll host laboratory summer camps. Teens will have the opportunity to visit professional laboratories and learn about the latest research on hair growth and anti-hair-loss technologies. They'll even get to participate in simple experiments under the guidance of scientists. This hands-on experience will not only deepen their understanding of hair-care science but also potentially spark their enthusiasm for scientific research.

Peers at College

We'll participate in prestigious competitions like the 2025 China Synthetic Biology Competition, the 2025 "Internet +" Innovation and Entrepreneurship Competition, and the Hangzhou Innovation and Entrepreneurship Competition. By taking part in these competitions, we can apply their academic knowledge to develop innovative anti-hair-loss solutions. Whether it's creating new products, business models, or marketing strategies, these competitions will serve as a platform for us to showcase our creativity and problem-solving abilities and perfect our projects.

Adults

Take Care of Your Hair

We're set to roll out an in-depth educational initiative in the form of a handbook. This handbook will be distributed at clinics and beauty salons, aiming to inform the adult population about the crucial role hair plays in their overall appearance and self-confidence.

In terms of practical hair care, the handbook will offer a wealth of useful tips. When it comes to choosing hair products, we'll guide adults on selecting the right shampoo and conditioner according to their specific hair types. For those with oily hair, a clarifying shampoo might be more suitable, while people with dry hair should opt for a moisturizing formula.

Public

These posters will be displayed in various public places such as schools, universities, hospitals, community centers, and shopping malls. The posters will feature attractive visuals and important information, like the benefits of using our products, common hair-loss myths debunked, and tips for maintaining healthy hair. This visual form of communication will help us quickly convey our message to the public and increase brand awareness.

Communication Tools

We'll utilize popular communication tools such as WeChat, Instagram, Twitter, official public accounts, and Xiaohongshu for our promotional efforts. On these platforms, we'll regularly post engaging content related to hair health, including tips, tricks, success stories, and the latest news about our anti-hair-loss products. We'll also interact with our followers, answering their questions and addressing their concerns. Through these social media channels, we can build a community of hair-conscious individuals, sharing knowledge and experiences, and promoting our brand as a trusted source of hair-care information.

News

We'll actively seek media coverage to promote our anti-hair-loss project. We'll issue press releases about our product launches, research breakthroughs, and educational initiatives. By collaborating with media outlets, we can reach a wider audience and gain credibility in the market. Media coverage can also help us address common public concerns about hair loss and our products, and provide accurate information to the public. This will enhance our brand image and increase public trust in our anti-hair-loss solutions.

Evaluation

We aim to establish a long-term educational effectiveness evaluation mechanism to continuously assess the impact of our educational initiatives in the anti-hair-loss project. This mechanism will help us understand the extent to which our target audiences, including students, adults, and the general public, have benefited from our educational efforts and identify areas for improvement.

Timed Follow - Ups

We will conduct regular and systematic follow-up surveys with the participants of our educational activities. For students who attended our workshops, summer camps, or engaged in competition-related projects, we'll set specific intervals for follow-up. For example, we'll reach out to them one month, three months, and six months after the activities. During these follow-ups, we'll inquire about whether they have applied the hair-care knowledge they learned in their daily lives. Have they changed their hair-washing habits? Are they more conscious of their diet in relation to hair health?

For adults who received our hair-care handbook at clinics or beauty salons, we'll also implement a follow-up plan. We'll contact them via email or phone at regular intervals to find out if the information in the handbook has been helpful. Did they start using different hair-care products based on the recommendations? Have they noticed any improvements in their hair condition? These follow-up efforts will provide us with valuable insights into the long-term impact of our educational content on their behavior and attitudes towards hair care.

Questionnaire Surveys

We'll design comprehensive questionnaire surveys to gather feedback from our target groups. The questionnaires will be distributed both online and offline. For the public who accessed our educational videos, posters, or social media content, we'll create an online survey accessible through platforms like WeChat, Instagram, and our official website. The survey will cover various aspects, such as the level of understanding of the educational content, whether it has influenced their perception of hair loss and prevention, and what additional information they would like to see.

For those who participated in more in-depth educational activities like the online teaching courses or the in-person interactions at clinics and beauty salons, we'll use more detailed questionnaires. These questionnaires will delve into their satisfaction with the teaching methods, the practicality of the knowledge and tips provided, and any suggestions for improving future educational offerings. By analyzing the data collected from these questionnaires, we can measure the effectiveness of our educational strategies, identify areas that need enhancement, and make data-driven decisions to optimize our future educational initiatives.
